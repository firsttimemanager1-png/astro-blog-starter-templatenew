# Promoted for performance. Expected to lead.

There is a fundamental challenge inside most organizations: managers are selected for individual performance, then expected to lead teams without practical training on how to actually do it.

The skills that made someone excellent as an individual contributor:
- focus
- execution
- technical depth

are not the same skills required to:
- develop teams
- motivate people
- coordinate organizations
- create accountability

This platform exists to bridge that gap through practical frameworks, operational systems, and honest lessons from experience.

# About the Platform

## Leadership lessons from scaling teams and operations

I've spent my career leading operations and scaling teams in fast growing organizations.

One pattern repeated itself:
Managers are expected to lead people without practical guidance on how to do it effectively.

The jump from individual contributor to manager is one of the hardest transitions in any career because the nature of the work changes entirely.

You stop doing and start enabling.

That shift requires an entirely different skill set.
